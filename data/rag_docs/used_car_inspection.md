# Used Car Inspection Checklist

Before buying a used car, inspect the vehicle in daylight and avoid rushing the decision.

## Exterior checks
Check for uneven paint, panel gaps, rust, dents, and signs of previous accident repair. Look at the alignment between doors, hood, bumper, and trunk. Uneven gaps may indicate body repair.

## Tires and suspension
Inspect tire wear. Uneven tire wear may suggest alignment, suspension, or chassis issues. During a test drive, listen for knocking sounds over bumps.

## Engine bay
Look for oil leaks, coolant leaks, burnt smells, loose wiring, and signs of poor maintenance. A very clean engine bay can also hide recent leak cleaning, so inspect carefully after a test drive.

## Interior and electronics
Test air conditioning, windows, dashboard lights, infotainment, locks, mirrors, parking sensors, and backup camera if available.

## Test drive
During the test drive, check acceleration, braking, steering, gear shifting, suspension noise, and dashboard warning lights. The car should track straight and brake without vibration.

## Documents
Verify registration papers, ownership history, insurance status, chassis/VIN number, service history, and whether there are unpaid fines or legal issues.

## Mechanic inspection
Always take a used car to a trusted mechanic before payment, especially if the car is high mileage, imported, modified, or suspiciously cheap.
